HomeFinder — Platform Overview

A real estate intelligence
platform built from scratch
for the buyer's side.

HomeFinder is a multi-tenant listing aggregator, scoring engine, and AI analyst that serves buyers, agents, and the platform owner from a single Flask application. This document explains the business model, the technical architecture, and your responsibilities as the platform owner.

Business Model

Three tiers, one purpose.

The platform operates on a three-tier model. You own the infrastructure. Agents are approved operators who bring buyers to the platform. Buyers are end users who get a buyer-only search tool unlike anything Zillow offers.

Your Role

What the platform owner does.

  • 🔐
    Agent Approval & Management Review agent applications from the Admin page. Approve, suspend, or reject agents. Each agent must verify their email and receive your approval before logging in.
  • 📊
    API Usage & Cost Monitoring The Metrics page tracks every API call by user: Zillow/Realtor fetches, Anthropic AI calls (deal, portfolio, preferences), and Google lookups. All-time and 30-day summaries with estimated costs. You control the API keys and bear the costs.
  • ⚙️
    Infrastructure & Deployment The app runs on IIS + waitress at toughguycomputing.com/home_finder_agents. You manage the server, SSL, IIS app pool, and deployment. Database is SQLite — single file, zero server process.
  • 🔑
    API Key Management You hold the RapidAPI key (Zillow + Realtor.com), Anthropic API key, and optional Google Maps key. These live in .env and are never exposed to agents or users.
  • 🗓
    Data Freshness The background scheduler fetches listings every 6 hours. You can trigger manual fetches. Monitor the digest for gaps or stale data, especially when your family's search timelines are active.
  • 👨‍👩‍👧
    Personal Search You are also the primary buyer on this platform — searching for a 4BR/3BA single-story retirement home in West Ashley, James Island, or Johns Island, under $600K, above flood plain, near a community pool.
