In Practice

What running a market looks like.

Week one in Denver.

You set up the Denver market — define zip codes for the metro area, set the map center, and name your target neighborhoods (Capitol Hill, Wash Park, Highlands, Stapleton). The pipeline runs overnight and pulls 280 active listings from Zillow and Realtor.com. By morning, every listing has a deal score.

You invite two local agents. They sign up, customize their branding, and start sending their buyer clients to the platform. One agent's client favorites eight listings and runs an AI portfolio analysis. The AI ranks them, flags a 120-day listing with two price cuts as the best negotiation opportunity, and suggests an opening offer $18K below ask.

From your admin dashboard you see the usage: 14 AI calls, 2 pipeline fetches, total estimated cost: $0.47. You check the feedback inbox — one buyer says "this is the best home search tool I've ever used." You approve a third agent.
